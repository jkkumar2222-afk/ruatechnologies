Microsoft 365 vs Google Workspace: how to choose

Rua Technologies delivers enterprise IT solutions including Microsoft 365, Azure, cybersecurity, cloud migration, managed IT services, and digital transformation tailored to modern businesses.

A practical comparison for teams deciding between the two most common productivity suites.

Choosing between Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace usually comes down to how your team already works, not which suite has more features on paper. Both cover email, file storage, video meetings, and document collaboration — the differences show up in daily use.

>

Microsoft 365

Desktop-first
  • Best for deep Excel, formulas, and macro-driven spreadsheet work
  • Best for Windows-based teams already on Active Directory
  • Best if Teams needs to double as your chat and calling hub
>

Google Workspace

Browser-first
  • Best for cloud-native teams that live in the browser
  • Best for real-time, native-feeling co-editing on documents
  • Best for smaller IT teams who want a simpler admin console

A closer look at each area

Collaboration style

Google Workspace apps are built browser-first, with real-time co-editing that feels native. Microsoft 365 apps are desktop-first with deep offline functionality and more advanced formatting, formulas, and macros — Excel in particular remains far more capable for complex spreadsheets and data work.

Email and storage

Gmail Business and Exchange Online both offer custom-domain business email with strong spam filtering. Storage allocation differs by plan, and both offer pooled storage models where higher-tier plans share a storage pool across the organization rather than a fixed per-user cap.

Video meetings

Google Meet and Microsoft Teams both support recording, screen sharing, and large participant counts on higher-tier plans. Teams has an edge if you also need a full internal chat and channel-based collaboration hub, since it's more deeply woven into the Microsoft 365 experience than Meet is into Workspace.

Security and admin controls

Both platforms offer strong baseline security, with more advanced options — data loss prevention, eDiscovery, device management — unlocked at higher plan tiers. Microsoft's stack has an edge for organizations that also use on-premises Active Directory or need deep integration with tools like Intune and Defender. Google's admin console is generally considered simpler for smaller IT teams.

Migration effort

Moving between the two platforms is a well-trodden path either direction, but mailbox and calendar migration always carries some risk of dropped data if not planned carefully — this is usually the single biggest reason organizations bring in a partner rather than doing it themselves.

The practical answer

Choose Microsoft 365 if…

Your team is already comfortable with Office file formats, needs advanced Excel work, or runs on Windows with Active Directory. It's usually the smoother fit.

Choose Google Workspace if…

Your team is cloud-native, browser-based, and values simplicity over deep formatting control. It tends to fit better for that kind of setup.

Running both? Many organizations in mixed environments run both platforms, using one for email and the other for specific collaboration needs — though this adds licensing complexity worth weighing against the benefit.
