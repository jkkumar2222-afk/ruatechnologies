What is Microsoft 365 Business?

Microsoft 365 Business is a cloud productivity and collaboration platform for organizations with up to 300 users. It combines business email, Microsoft Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint, Office applications, security services and device management into a single subscription designed for small and medium-sized businesses.

How Licensing Works

  • Licenses are assigned per user.
  • Each licensed user can sign in across multiple supported devices.
  • Plans can be mixed to match different employee roles.
  • Businesses can upgrade from Basic to Standard or Premium as requirements evolve.

Microsoft 365 Business Basic Entry-level

The entry-level business subscription for organizations that want professional email, Microsoft Teams, OneDrive and SharePoint without desktop Office applications — ideal for startups, frontline workers and web-first teams.

Included Services

  • Exchange Online business email
  • Microsoft Teams
  • OneDrive (1 TB per user)
  • SharePoint Online
  • Word, Excel & PowerPoint for the web
  • Microsoft Forms and Planner

Advantages

  • Lowest licensing cost
  • Cloud-first collaboration
  • Business email and calendars
  • Secure file sharing
  • Ideal for remote teams

Limitations

  • No desktop Office apps
  • No Microsoft Intune
  • No Microsoft Defender for Business
  • Limited advanced security features

Best Fit: Startups, retail businesses, hospitality, field teams and organizations that mainly rely on browser-based applications while keeping licensing costs under control.

Microsoft 365 Business Standard Most Popular

Builds on Business Basic by adding the full desktop versions of Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook and other Office applications for teams that need powerful productivity plus cloud collaboration.

What's Included

  • Desktop Office apps
  • Exchange Online
  • Teams
  • OneDrive & SharePoint
  • Outlook desktop
  • Publisher & Access (Windows)

Advantages

  • Work online and offline
  • Professional document creation
  • Better collaboration
  • Supports hybrid work
  • Excellent value

Limitations

  • No Intune
  • No Defender for Business
  • No Conditional Access
  • Limited advanced security

Best Fit: Accounting firms, consultants, engineering companies, educational institutions, professional services and growing SMBs that depend on Office desktop applications every day.

Recommendation

For most organizations, Business Standard offers the best balance of productivity, collaboration and licensing cost.

Microsoft 365 Business Premium Most Secure

Combines the complete productivity experience of Business Standard with advanced security, identity protection and endpoint management — built to secure users, devices and data while enabling modern hybrid work.

Security Features

  • Microsoft Defender for Business
  • Microsoft Intune
  • Microsoft Entra ID capabilities
  • Conditional Access
  • Endpoint protection
  • Device management

Business Benefits

  • Zero Trust foundation
  • Protection from phishing & ransomware
  • Centralized device administration
  • Secure remote work
  • Compliance readiness

Best For

  • Healthcare
  • Finance
  • Legal firms
  • Professional services
  • Growing SMBs

Rua Technologies Recommendation

Choose Business Premium if cybersecurity, compliance and centralized endpoint management are priorities. It delivers the best long-term value for businesses protecting sensitive information.

Common Licensing Mistakes

Overbuying

Purchasing Premium for every employee when only specific roles require advanced security.

Ignoring Security

Choosing the lowest-cost plan without considering compliance or cyber risks.

No Growth Plan

Selecting a plan that cannot support future AI, remote work and device management needs.

Buying Recommendation

Review business requirements, security obligations and growth plans before selecting licenses. A mixed-license strategy often delivers the best balance of cost and functionality.
